about

I have spent twelve years building the systems that move money: trading platforms, payment integrations, API gateways, order management systems, and the parts of fintech that never make it into a demo but decide whether the company survives its first year.

I was the first technical hire at a digital lending company, where I built the integration layer with Indian banking systems, set up the data infrastructure, and built an information security practice from zero. Later, I led engineering on a cryptocurrency trading platform that went from nothing to real volume in under two months and survived a regulatory wallet lockdown a few months after launch. I have also led integration programmes inside large enterprises, and I have been the person a team called when the thing they built under pressure started breaking.

None of this happened calmly. Most of it happened at odd hours, with strong chai, and a team that trusted me enough to tell me when I was wrong. I am still that person, just with better sleep now.

Right now I work as a fractional CTO and advisor with a small number of early-stage fintech and B2B founders. I am also open to senior engineering leadership roles, including VP of Engineering, Director of Engineering, and Head of Engineering, where the work matches the convictions below.

what I actually believe

01

Practices outlast products.

The system you ship matters less than whether your team can operate it on Monday morning.

02

MVP and a sustainable team are both non-negotiable.

Skip either one, and you do not get to the second iteration.

03

Customer support is a tech function.

If your engineers do not know what is breaking for users, you are flying blind.

04

Security and observability are not features you add later.

When you handle real money, they are the cost of being allowed to do the work at all.

05

Tech is an enabler, never a magic wand.

It compresses time and effort on pain points that already exist. It does not solve organisational, market, or compliance problems.
